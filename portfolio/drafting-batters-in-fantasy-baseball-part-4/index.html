Drafting Batters in Fantasy Baseball, Part 4

End of Season Results

Team Expectations vs. Reality

Up until this point, we’ve been looking at stat projections for 2018, but now that the season is over, we can see just how these projections played out. Let’s bring in the final 2018 stats from Fangraphs, and compare them to the projected stats. I’ve renamed all final stats to include the f_ prefix. While I’m at it, I also calculated z-scores for each stat, and over the total. Finally, I merged this dataset to the full batters dataset, and also to the subset of players I drafted.

end_bat_z <- read.csv("../data/post1/batters_final.csv") %>%
  rename(f_R = R,
         f_HR = HR,
         f_RBI = RBI,
         f_SO = SO,
         f_SB = SB,
         f_OPS = OPS,
         f_WAR = WAR) %>%
  filter(PA >= 300) %>%
  mutate(f_R_z = z_score(f_R),
         f_HR_z = z_score(f_HR),
         f_RBI_z = z_score(f_RBI),
         f_SO_z = -z_score(f_SO),
         f_SB_z = z_score(f_SB),
         f_OPS_z = z_score(f_OPS),
         f_tot_z = round((f_R_z + f_HR_z + f_RBI_z + f_SO_z + f_SB_z + f_OPS_z), 3),
         playerid = as.character(playerid)) %>%
  select(-Team)
  
all_final <- inner_join(end_bat_z, bat_z, by = c("playerid", "Name"))
drafted_final <- inner_join(end_bat_z, drafted, by = c("Name"))

Lessons Learned

Now that I’ve done the post-mortem, here are the top three takeaways from my exploration: 1) Don’t rely solely on WAR to draft players. 2) Use data, not personal bias (namely, stop over-drafting Giants players) 3) Pay attention to positional talent – draft outfielders earlier and catchers later.

I also did a little bit of exploration in my own league, and I found that most people don’t draft catchers in the first 10 rounds, because catchers don’t seem to make much of a difference.

Yeah, this was news to me!
